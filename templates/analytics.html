Top Terms per Genre

From the analysis of 2500 books which is a subset of dataset compiled by Inamdar of books from GoodReads scraped using Selenium.
Checkout the compiled dataset in Kaggle. Link here.

TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) values reflect how important the words are on the collection of documents or dataset.

For the fantasy genre, we can see that the first six entries represent the names of protagonists in a fantasy story; and from the terms “goddess”, “demigod”, combined with names of different god and goddesses from Greek mythology (Gaea, Zeus, Aphrodite), and “Percy”, “Jackson”, which when combined is a character from a book that is said to be a son of Poseidon, most themes of fantasy books tackles about mythology.

In history genre, all entries point out mostly to terms such as “confidential”, “interrogate”, “American”, “tension”, “franklin”, and “benjamin”. From these top terms, it can pertain to the history of America as its Americans are stated and when combined as Benjamin Franklin the latter terms, it points out to the founding father of United States. It can be concluded that most of the frequent terms pertain to history about politics or politics in general, the government, or the state.

Most terms include descriptions of the supernatural since frequent terms such as “invisible”, “invisibility”, and “mysterious” appear frequently. A pattern can also be observed here since there are also entries of “town”, “house”, “village”, and “landlady”, “lodging”, and “inn” which all portrays a typical medieval horror story setting. One term in particular, “griffin”, top term seems to characterize a fictional entity which mostly occurs in a particular type of horror book. Terms invoking fear such as “terrorize”, “terror”, “betray” are all typical to a horror story.

There is no observable pattern within the top 20 terms but involves a lot of terms associated with the concept of themes of law enforcement, security, conflict, and the consequences of criminal behavior based on the words, “guard”, “prison”, “prisoner”, “suspect”, “criminal”, “military”, and “war”. Occasionally, it also involves scientific terms including “experiment” and “wormhole”.

Entries are mixed up with names, occupation or relationships, terminologies relating to crime and prosecution, and settings. Terms such as “murder”, “evidence”, “custody”, “framed”, “sentenced”, “possession”, and “goal” can be associated with crimes and law enforcement. Meanwhile, terms “outside” and “prison” are mostly the settings in thriller books. But overall, there is no definite pattern observed within Thriller books except that the majority of the books can be characterized with terms related to investigation, committing, and prosecuting a crime.

The terms above may not be a representation for ALL existing fantasy, history, horror, science, and thriller books . The terms above are JUST the most frequent and relevant terms from the 2500 books included in the dataset. New terms may be added and values of the listed terms' frequency (TF) and relevance (IDF) may change when more data are added.
